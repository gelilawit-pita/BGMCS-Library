Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

You can register for a free library card at any of our branches with valid photo ID and proof of address. You can also begin the process online through our website. Library cards are available to all residents of Bookville and surrounding areas. Children under 18 need a parent or guardian's signature.

On most holidays, we're open from 2am to 9pm. We're closed on Ephiphany, Meskel, Christmas, Ester, and New Year's Day. Check our website for specific holiday schedules. Summer hours (June-August) may vary, with extended hours on weekdays.

Yes! Log in to your account on our website or mobile app to renew items. Most materials can be renewed twice if there are no holds on them. Some special collection items may have different renewal policies. You'll receive email reminders before items are due.

We have 12 study rooms available on a first-come, first-served basis. Rooms can be reserved in advance by library card holders for up to 2 hours per day. Study rooms include whiteboards, power outlets, and some have computer hookups. Group study rooms accommodate 4-8 people.

Regular patrons can borrow up to 30 items at once, including up to 10 DVDs and 5 video games. Teachers with educator cards may borrow up to 50 items. There are no limits on e-books and digital audiobooks through our OverDrive/Libby service.

Yes, we offer free notary services at our main branch. Please call ahead to confirm notary availability as hours vary. Bring your unsigned document and valid photo ID. Notaries cannot notarize wills, real estate documents, or documents in languages they don't understand.

We accept donations of gently used books, DVDs, and CDs during regular hours. We cannot accept textbooks, encyclopedias, magazines, or damaged items. Donations may be added to our collection or sold in our Friends of the Library book sales to support library programs.

Our community meeting room seats up to 50 people and is available for free to non-profit groups when not in use for library programs. Reservations can be made up to 3 months in advance. Commercial groups may rent the space for a small fee. Kitchen facilities are available.

About Us

Established in 1997, the BGMCS Bahir Dar School Library supports students and teachers with a quiet reading space, textbooks, storybooks, and helpful reference materials. It plays a key role in encouraging learning and a love for reading.
